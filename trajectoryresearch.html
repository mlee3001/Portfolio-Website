Trajectory Dynamics Research

GitHub Repository Link

This research is focused on the study of interaction kernels for interaction dynamics. The paper "Nonparametric inference of interaction laws in systems of agents from trajectory data", by Fei Lu et al, was primarily used as a basis for this research. Using algorithms to compute learning matricies and solve linear systems in order to investigate the potential source interaction kernel for a dynamic system. The links to the paper and associated appendix can be found below.

Opinion Dynamics

2D Opinion Dynamics Simulation

The above opinion dynamics simulations were created using the interaction kernel from the research appendix. This simulation represents the attraction influence of people on each other and how consensus can form. The method used for simulation and the subsequent learning of the source interaction kernel is dimensionally independent and thus the 1 dimensional and 2 dimensional simulations are shown here to represent that.

1D Reverse Opinion Dynamics Simulation

The simulation above has been obtained by attempting to determine the interaction kernel governing the simulation by utilizing the following algorithm:

This algorithm determines coefficients for basis functions that are then used to recreate the opinion dynamics simulation. This new simulation is then compared to one of the trajectories used to learn the kernel to determine success of the algorithm. The strength of the kernel at varying pairwise distances (r) are also compared to evaluate the algorithm.

Currently the least squares algorithm over estimates the coefficients for the basis functions. This is what results in the fast condensation of the individual particles along with the significant differential between the two kernels.

Lennard-Jones Dynamics

1D Lennard-Jones Simulation

2D Lennard-Jones Simulation

These simulations are generated in a similar manner to the opinion dynamics simulations. The main difference comes from the function of the governing kernel. In the Lennard-Jones simulation, the particles repulse instead of attract. This type of simulation is mainly used in particle simulations that represent atomic systems since the kernel can represent the Pauli repulsion force and the van der Waals force depending on the pairwise distance.
